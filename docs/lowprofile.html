How To Keep A Low Profile

Find the best tips to avoid being tracked


Cindy Gonzalez

Nov 18 2020

ABOUT THE AUTHOR:
Cindy Gonzalez is studying Human-Computer Interaction as an incoming freshman at the New Jersey Institute of Technology.

How can you protect yourself from browser fingerprinting?

Maybe you have heard that there are browsers out there like firefox 72 which has a browser fingerprinting protection implemented in the system, for example you can choose to have cookies automatically deleted after each session. You can also manually clean your internet browsing history after every use. Another thing you can do is read carefully! Sometimes we are asked to give more information that we really need by apps permission requests. And consider whether it's worth it for you to give so much information when you are visiting a website by evaluating your purpose in the website. Remember that some people are just trying to collect as much information from you as they can.

Detour browsers are also commonly used. You can also use plugins to block javascript, but this often makes your navigating experience a lot more complicated since all websites use javascript nowadays, so this option might not be worth it. You can also keep your antivirus up to date up to date in case you run into a malicious website. Make sure you scan files with your antivirus software before downloading. And of course, don’t reuse passwords for multiple websites. You can also turn on your browser's pop up blocker.

To avoid IP tracking you can use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) tool when working on your personal computer. For example IP Vanish where you log into the VPN as an intermediary.

You can also double check that you are using HTTPS or Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) if you are providing personal information, making purchases online, or you want to verify that you are in a secure and trustable website. This is found as “https://” in your browsers URL bar.

What can you really do to avoid tracking?

You might have already inferred that avoiding tracking is practically impossible, but the options above might help you to secure that less of your personal information gets tracked. What can really prevent you from being tracked is to keep a low profile:

Don’t be so unique! We know this is not what you often hear, but it is better to blend in when browsing through the internet. For example, use browsers that most people use, like Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge instead of a super unique browser, because that will only make it easier for you to be identified. Also, try to avoid crazy plugins, these are also easy to be tracked, and therefore easier for anyone to make your browser fingerprint more identifiable. When you keep a low profile you become less predictable and less identifiable! Which prevents you and your computer from being targeted.

Remember to spread the word out, awareness is a powerful tool and the more people who grow concerned about privacy, the easier it would be for us to fight for it.
